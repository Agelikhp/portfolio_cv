My project for the "Sculptural Instruments & Sound Art Exhibition" at Aalto University in November 2018 showcased a unique blend of art and technology, exploring the auditory experience of metal surfaces. The installation was constructed primarily from metal and wood, utilizing welding techniques to create a robust structure. The choice of wood was integral for the internal mechanism, which facilitated the interaction between the audience and the artwork.

This experience familiarized me with woodworking tools and machinery that I had never used before, including large machines and welding equipment. I am grateful to Aalto University for providing this opportunity to expand my skill set in constructing artworks.

Key Features of my Project:

Sensors: Strategically placed to detect the presence of passersby, triggering the installation to play a melody.

Sound Production: The melody produced emerged from the act of scratching the metal surface, introducing an intriguing contrast between sound and beauty.

Conceptual Exploration: Examined themes of discomfort and surprise, transforming potentially bothersome sounds into melodic experiences.

Dimensions: 1.25cm x 2.00cm, materials: metal, wood, Arduino Software & Hardware.

Through this project, I not only developed my artistic vision but also gained practical experience in construction techniques that enhanced my creative practice.
